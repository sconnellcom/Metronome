How to Use

Modes

  • Regular: Visual pulse with sound. Standard metronome behavior.
  • Silent: Visual pulse only, no sound. Practice staying on tempo visually.
  • Auto: Motion detection mode. The metronome beeps ONLY when you go off-beat.

Auto Mode (Motion Detection)

How it works:

  • The metronome runs silently in the background
  • Your device detects when you tap/move (e.g., tapping your phone, moving to the beat)
  • System checks TWO things:
    • Timing: Is your tap within the "Timing Tolerance" (in milliseconds) of the metronome beat?
    • Tempo: Is your detected BPM within the "BPM Tolerance" of the target tempo?
  • If BOTH timing and tempo are within tolerance → You're ON BEAT (no sound, green status)
  • If EITHER timing or tempo is off → You're OFF BEAT (count increases)
  • After 6 consecutive OFF BEAT detections → Metronome starts beeping to guide you back
  • Metronome will beep for at least 6 beats once it starts
  • After 6 consecutive ON BEAT detections → Beeping stops

Auto Mode Settings

  • Beat Detection (0-100%): How sensitive motion detection is. Higher % = easier to detect light taps. Lower % = only detects harder hits. Default: 50%
  • Timing Tolerance (5-1000ms): How many milliseconds off you can be and still count as "on beat".
    • 100ms = must be within 0.1 seconds of metronome beat
    • Lower = stricter, harder to stay on beat
    • Higher = more forgiving
  • BPM Tolerance (0-20): How many BPM off you can be and still count as "on beat".
    • 5 BPM = your tempo can be 115-125 BPM when target is 120 BPM
    • 0 = must match target BPM exactly
    • Higher = more forgiving for tempo drift

Tip: Want to focus on just one metric? Set the other tolerance very high!

  • Focus on tempo only: Set Timing Tolerance to 1000ms (max)
  • Focus on timing only: Set BPM Tolerance to 20 (max)

Display Info

  • Status: Shows if you're "On beat", "Off beat", or detecting motion
  • Detected BPM: Your actual tapping tempo (calculated from your beats)
  • Accuracy: How many milliseconds off you are from the metronome beat

Quick Tempo Selection

Click the large BPM number to open a menu with common musical tempo presets (Largo, Adagio, Andante, Moderato, Allegretto, Allegro, Vivace, Presto).

Sound Options

Choose from Beep, Bass Drum, Cymbal, Tock, drum riffs (4-count or 8-count patterns), or Vibrate (vibrates the device instead of playing a sound)

Screen Wake Lock

The screen stays on while the metronome is running to prevent interruptions during practice.
